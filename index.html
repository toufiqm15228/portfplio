001 — Profile

Mapping species, land & landscapes

I'm a GIS professional based in Dhaka with over two years of experience across spatial data analysis, field surveys and biodiversity-focused mapping. My work bridges raw field data — GPS points, Kobo forms, drone footage — with satellite imagery to produce evidence that holds up in research and policy contexts.

I specialise in species distribution mapping, EOO & AOO analysis aligned with IUCN Red List standards, and habitat suitability modelling using MaxEnt, Random Forest and deep learning approaches (CNN, RNN, CA-Markov). I've applied this across haor ecosystems, coastal agriculture and urban land zoning.

Currently an Assistant GIS Analyst at Sheltech Consultants, supporting field-level spatial data collection for the UDD Land Zoning Project. Previously, I worked across UNDP-funded haor resilience research, precision agriculture, and biodiversity surveys in Sylhet, Kishoreganj, Sunamganj and Cox's Bazar.

Good geospatial work starts in the field — a coordinate is only as accurate as the survey that recorded it.
